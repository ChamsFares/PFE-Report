\hspace{1.5em} Cloud computing, hyperscale facilities, and AI have revolutionized our modern-day infrastructures. AI requires tremendous amounts of bandwidth along with extremely low latency and flexibility, which are impossible to achieve through traditional designs. Data Centers have become the backbone of today’s digital economy, with the network fabric dictating the success or failure of such an undertaking.

The three-tier architecture that has been used widely since time immemorial was designed keeping north-south traffic and isolated single-site facilities in mind. It suffers from several weaknesses today, which include wasted bandwidth due to Spanning Trees, instability when extending Layer 2 across multiple locations, VM mobility across site boundaries is hindered, and manual management is difficult to sustain. Such problems pose very real limits to the network performance of a national company such as Tunisie Telecom and thus represent an important reason for the initiation of this project.

The proposal for this project entails the implementation of a BGP EVPN-VXLAN IP fabric at multiple locations using the Cisco Nexus 9000 family of devices and its management through NDFC.

This report is organized into four chapters:

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Chapter 1 Project Framework:}
Host organization, existing limitations, project scope and methodology.
\item \textbf{Chapter 2 Technological Foundations:}
Evolution from classical networking to IP Fabrics, Spine-Leaf, VXLAN, and BGP EVPN.
\item \textbf{Chapter 3 Automation and Architecture Design:}
NDFC orchestration, multi-site fabric design, underlay/overlay provisioning, security.
\item \textbf{Chapter 4 Implementation and Validation:}
EVE-NG deployment, eight test scenarios validating VM mobility, automation, and resilience.
\end{itemize}

Together, they constitute a comprehensive engineering
experience, from an actual practical challenge to a
solution that adheres to industry standards. This work
is a direct result of the needs of one of the most
important telecoms in Tunisia.
